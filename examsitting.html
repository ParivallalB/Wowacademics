Exam Sitting Advice

ACCA has exam four times in a year, not taking into account the CBE. You should make a plan to take the flexibility of exams before you starting to study.

The four exam sessions are as follows:.

Session March June September December
Exam Date 1st week of March 1st week of June 1st week of September 1st week of December
Result Date Mid of April Mid of July Mid of October Mid of January
  • Papers F1-F4 are Computer based exams (CBE) that can be taken at any time of the year
  • Papers F5-P7 are paper based exams & has four sittings per year
  • We recommend you aim & complete four subjects per year. This will ensure that you clear ACCA in 3.5 years

We would recommend the following study plans for you

  • Plan 1: One at a Time
  • We would say this is the best option as you will be devoting “FULL THREE MONTHS” for studying one subject alone. This will make you to concentrate & increase your understanding of the subject. Each subject has its own learning style for example F5 – Management accounting has number crunching & theory with less importance of memorizing, whereas F4 Corporate and Business Law has full of theory & a memory intensive exam. So it’s important that you are in a rhythm to study each different subject. Following this plan will give you ACCA time plan like the below table.
    • Exam Session Paper Paper Paper Paper
    March F1 F5 F9 P optional paper 1
    June F2 F6 P1 P optional paper 2
    September F3 F7 P2
    December F4 F8 P3
    First year Second year Third year Fourth Year
  • Good thing
    • You focus on a one paper only
    • You will get into a pattern of study
  • Not so Good thing
    • You might feel exhausted as you have to study throughout the year.
    • You have to start studying for the next exam before you know the results for the previous exam you wrote
  • Plan 2: Combine – Your way
  • In this approach you juggle up exam based on your ability & need for relaxation. For example, if you like number crunching a lot then you could take up F5 & F9 they are easy for you. You could take up a theory exam like F8 in one exam window. Following this plan will give you an ACCA time plan like the below table.
    • Exam Session Paper Paper Paper
    March F1 F8 P3
    June F2 & F3 F7 & F9 P optional paper 1
    September F4 P1 P optional paper 2
    December F5 & F6 P2
    First year Second year Third year
  • Good thing
    • You could finish ACCA quickly
  • Not so Good thing
    • But there is a possibility of getting too bored with studies
    • You might not fully learn & absorb the subject. This leads to difficulty when studying higher level papers
    • You have to start studying for the next exam before you know the results for the previous exam you wrote
We would like to suggest a few things before taking up exam, please consider,
  • Other courses – If you are studying for other courses. Try to use the flexible 4 exam window & CBEs to your benefit.
  • Arrears – Do you have any uncompleted paper? Or if you have failed any previous papers please don’t take up tough new papers.
  • Workload – Take up easy papers when your exams clash with the busy time of your office
  • Personal – Take into account your holidays, family events when planning for an exam
  • Company – Confirm if your company would give study holidays or some kind of reduced workload during your studies
